2 The RFI Priority Matrix

A printable classification system. Teach your team to triage in under 30 seconds.

Urgent

⚠ Must act today

Definition: Safety, structural integrity, or regulatory non-compliance. Requires stop-work or immediate approval.

Examples: Rebar clash with MEP penetration • Load rating discrepancy on shoring • Fire-rated assembly not matching approved drawings • OSHA violation risk

Target response: < 24 hours  |  Escalate: Directly to design lead + GC PM

High

⏱ Schedule-critical

Definition: Affects the next activity in the critical path. Creates a stand-by condition if unresolved beyond 48 hours.

Examples: Undefined slab edge condition ahead of pour • Missing embedment specs before steel delivery • MEP rough-in conflict that blocks drywall • Grade discrepancy affecting earthwork

Target response: < 48 hours  |  Escalate: To discipline engineer + superintendent

Medium

⚙ Design clarification

Definition: Tolerable with minor delay. Can be resolved within normal submittal cycles without stopping work.

Examples: Dimension clarification on non-structural element • Finish schedule conflict between drawings • Valve specification substitution • Typical detail vs. site condition mismatch

Target response: < 5 business days  |  Escalate: To discipline engineer (normal channel)

Low

💡 Informational

Definition: Nice-to-have clarification. No impact on schedule, cost, or safety. Can be batched.

Examples: Request for as-built verification • Preference inquiry on approved equal product • Record-keeping RFI • Future-phase coordination question

Target response: < 10 business days  |  Escalate: Batch for weekly review

Pro tip: Print this matrix and post it at your trailer whiteboard. Have your foreman & APM tag every new RFI with U / H / M / L before it enters the log.

AI shortcut: Workflow 1 (next page) auto-classifies every incoming RFI using this exact matrix — zero training required.

3 Quick-Deploy AI Workflows

Copy-paste these prompts today. Each workflow takes under 30 minutes to set up.

WORKFLOW 1

⚡ Auto-Classify Incoming RFI Urgency

⏰ Setup: 10 min ✓ Saves: 4–6 hrs/week

📄 What it does

Paste any incoming RFI description (from email, Procore, or field note) and get an instant urgency classification: Urgent, High, Medium, or Low — using the Priority Matrix from page 2. Includes a 1-sentence rationale so you can verify before assigning.

📝 Exact Prompt (copy-paste into ChatGPT, Claude, or your preferred AI)

You are an expert construction project engineer. Classify the following RFI using the Priority Matrix below. Return ONLY the priority level (Urgent / High / Medium / Low) plus one short reason sentence. PRIORITY MATRIX: - Urgent: Safety hazard, structural integrity, regulatory non-compliance, stop-work required. Respond <24h. - High: Schedule-critical, affects next activity on critical path. Respond <48h. - Medium: Design clarification, acceptable with minor delay. Respond <5 business days. - Low: Informational, nice-to-have, no schedule impact. Batch for weekly review. RFI DESCRIPTION: [Paste RFI description here] Respond in this format: Priority: [Level] Why: [Single sentence rationale]

✓ Expected result

Within 30 seconds you get a priority level like “High — Affects the MEP rough-in sequence ahead of drywall, which is on the critical path.” Assign the right owner immediately instead of spending 5 minutes reading and debating.

🛠 Time to implement

10 minutes. Open your AI chat, copy the prompt above, paste your first RFI. That’s it. Optional: create a shortcut / saved prompt in your AI tool so you don’t have to paste the full matrix every time.

Tip: Save this as a reusable prompt template in your AI tool. Name it “RFI Classifier.”

3 Quick-Deploy AI Workflows

Workflow 2 — The one that saves you the most typing time.

WORKFLOW 2

✍ Draft RFI Response with Spec Reference

⏰ Setup: 20 min ✓ Saves: 6–10 hrs/week

📄 What it does

Take an incoming RFI question and instantly draft a professional response that references the correct specification section, drawing detail, or standard. Cuts your per-RFI writing time from 15–20 minutes down to 2 minutes — you just verify and hit send.

📝 Exact Prompt (copy-paste)

You are a senior construction project engineer. Draft a professional RFI response based on the details below. Reference the relevant specification section, drawing number, or standard if provided. Use a firm but collaborative tone. Keep it under 200 words. RFI QUESTION: [Paste the RFI question here] RELEVANT CONTRACT DOCUMENTS (paste any that apply): - Spec Section: [e.g., 03 30 00 - Cast-in-Place Concrete] - Drawing: [e.g., S-402, Detail 5] - Standard: [e.g., ACI 318-19] Designer/Consultant Name: [Name] Contractor POC: [Name] Write the response as if you are speaking on behalf of the design team. Start with a clear answer (Yes/No/As Noted/Revise). Follow with the supporting reference. Close with a proposed next step. Do NOT include legal disclaimers or “subject to review” language that adds ambiguity.

✓ Expected result

A clean, spec-backed response like: “Yes — per Spec Section 03 30 00, para 3.5.A, the remedy pour is acceptable provided the surface is prepared per ACI 301. Proceed with the detail shown on S-402 Detail 5. Submit revised shop drawing for record.” Ready to send after a 30-second review.

🛠 Time to implement

20 minutes. Create a project-specific version by pasting the relevant spec section numbers and drawing indexes into the prompt template. Save as a project-level GPT or custom Claude instruction for one-click use.

Pro move: Build a shared prompt with your top 10 most-used spec sections pre-loaded.

3 Quick-Deploy AI Workflows

Workflow 3 — Impress stakeholders without the Sunday-night scramble.

WORKFLOW 3

📊 Generate Weekly RFI Status Summary

⏰ Setup: 15 min ✓ Saves: 2–4 hrs/week

📄 What it does

Feed your raw RFI log (export from Procore, Bluebeam, or your tracker) into this prompt and get a polished executive summary: total open/closed, aging breakdown, top blockers, priority distribution, and a recommended action plan — formatted for owner meetings or weekly reports.

📝 Exact Prompt (copy-paste)

You are a construction project controls specialist. Generate a weekly RFI status summary from the raw log below. Format it for a stakeholder meeting (owners, GC, design team). Use clear sections with bullet points. RAW RFI LOG (export columns: RFI#, Date, Description, Priority, Status, Days Open, Assigned To): [Paste your RFI log CSV or table here] GENERATE THE FOLLOWING SECTIONS: 1. OVERVIEW: Total active, opened this week, closed this week, net change. 2. AGING: Count by bucket — <7 days, 7–14 days, 15–21 days, >21 days. Flag any over 14 days. 3. PRIORITY DISTRIBUTION: Count of Urgent / High / Medium / Low currently open. 4. TOP 3 BLOCKERS: The longest-open or highest-priority RFIs that need executive attention. Include RFI#, days open, and a 1-line summary. 5. ACTION ITEMS: For each blocker, recommend a specific next step and owner. 6. TREND: Compare this week to last week (open count, aged items) if last week's data is included. Use a professional, neutral tone. No markdown tables — use plain text or bullet lists.

✓ Expected result

A ready-to-present weekly status in under 60 seconds. Example: “27 open RFIs (flat vs last week), 8 opened this week, 6 closed. 3 items over 21 days — RFI-214 (Structural slab edge, 34 days) is the #1 blocker. Recommend: PM to escalate to structural engineer by Wednesday.”

🛠 Time to implement

15 minutes. Export your RFI log as CSV. Paste into the prompt. Save the export + prompt combo as a recurring Friday-afternoon routine. Set a calendar reminder for 30 minutes — you’ll be done in 10.

💡 All three workflows together save you 12–20 hours per week. That’s 1.5–2.5 full working days returned to proactive project management. The ROI on 45 minutes of setup time is immediate.
